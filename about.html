Abstract

We investigate the equity market timing hypothesis of capital structure in the case of Vietnam. The two questions that we aim to answer in this paper are: Whether or not Vietnamese listed non-financial firmsâ€™ capital structures are affected by equity market timing? If they are indeed affected, does the effect last throughout the years, or is it only a short-term effect? The results suggest that capital structure is the cumulative outcomes of past market timing attempts for Vietnamese non-financial firms. Our findings are consistent with the theory proposed by Baker & Wurgler (2002), the pioneering research whose model have been widely adopted in numerous studies around the world in this field.
